GoodreadsCase Study

Summary

A redesign of the bookshelf page of the goodreads website. I started from scratch with a new logo and completely redesigned the page from there. It is an extremely cluttered site in general, so I decided to give the page more space by organizing it's content into menus and put the focus on the book cover art. After all, a site all about reading should inspire just that!

Challenges and Solutions

The biggest challenge with this project was getting an idea for the general aesthetic I wanted the page to have. I struggled with direction, so I started sketching ideas for a new logo. I tested them out in Illustrator and finally came to one I liked after prototyping the navigation bar in HTML and CSS. It wasn't until then that I got a sense of what the page should feel like.

I also noticed that I wasn't inspired to read when I was on the site. Cleaning up all of the unnecessary verbage and putting the book covers in the limelight really helped with that.
